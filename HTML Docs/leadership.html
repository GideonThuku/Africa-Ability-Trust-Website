Our Leadership

Beryl Ogutu

CO-EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR & TRUSTEE
beryl@africiability.org

Beryl Lung’ayi Ogutu is a passionate special needs educator and community leader whose vision gave birth to Africa Ability Trust. She holds a degree in Special Needs Education (Visually Impaired) from Moi University and is trained in Kenyan Sign Language and Braille.

Her experience spans inclusive teaching, community disability advocacy, and government service, including roles at the Kenya Institute of Special Education (KISE), National Council for Persons with Disabilities (NCPWD), Kibos Special School for the Visually Impaired, Kefjoy Academy and the Office of the Nominated Senator for Persons With Disabilities (PWDs). Beryl also served with African Christian Outreach, where she initiated its first disability program.

Beryl is also a dedicated foster parent to children with disabilities, an experience that deepens her empathy and commitment to inclusive care. In her personal time, she enjoys traditional dancing, performing arts, and farming.

Gideon Thuku

CO-EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR & TRUSTEE
gideon@africiability.org

Gideon is a passionate community mobilizer, disability rights advocate, and tech enthusiast with a background in economics, statistics, and multimedia production. He is also an aspiring AI engineer, eager to explore how technology can transform advocacy, ministry, and community empowerment.

Since 2014, he has served with African Christian Outreach, where he initiated its first disability program. Gideon has been involved in several Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) in community development projects. Through his experience, he observed a critical gap in disability inclusion within community organizations, churches, and broader community structures.

Gideon is a devoted foster parent to children with disabilities, a skilled media creator, and a recreational marathon runner. He aims to use his love for running as a platform to raise awareness and funds for disability inclusion.
